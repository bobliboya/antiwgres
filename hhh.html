Outline:
The APB is an algebra based physics course that prepares students for college
level algebra based physics courses.  The APB program is an advanced program
that allows students to have a competitive transcript for college applications.  
Also, this course will help the student prepare for doing well on the APB
examination.  The course covers: Kinematics, Newton's Laws of Motion,
Work/Energy And Power, Linear Momentum And Impulse, Uniform Circular
Motion, Rotational Dynamics, And Oscillations And Gravitation, Waves, and
Electrostatics, basic circuits, etc..

Delivery:
This course should meet no less than 5 hours a week (physics is one of the most
difficult subjects to master, as it uses advanced mathematical techniques
combined with philosophy of nature, so please be focussed).   We will have lab
experiments, challenges, and projects throughout the course to help connect the
theory to practical use of the knowledge. The course will comprise of lectures,
student on student teaching, students coming to the board to solve problems,
and more.

Assessment:
The students will assessed in various ways.  The student will be given homework,
called upon in class, given quizzes, in addition to midterm and semester exams.
We will also use technology in class that will allow the teacher to get immediate
feedback on student understanding.

Textbook:
Rice University, College Physics, OpenStax College

Other Materials:
You will need to download Microsoft OneNote to use in class.  This may require
you to open a Microsoft email account.  Also, I will use Mathematica from time to
time in class.  If you want, you can purchase your own copy of Mathematica if you
are interested in learning how to use Mathematica to program the models used in
class. Also, if you are not able to download OneNote, you will need a dedicated
notebook for taking notes in class and taking down data for labs.
